# HR Resume Review Reference

Use this reference for resume critique, rewriting, and new resume creation.

## Review Dimensions

Score mentally across these dimensions before rewriting:

- Target fit: the top third must make the target role obvious within 8 seconds.
- Relevance: strongest and most recent evidence should come before weaker or older material.
- Achievement density: bullets should show scope, method, and result, not only duties.
- Credibility: dates, employers, schools, awards, and metrics must be internally consistent.
- Readability: sections should be scannable, concise, and free of long paragraphs.
- Keyword coverage: include target-role tools, methods, domain nouns, and responsibility verbs.
- Risk control: remove exaggerated claims, private data, irrelevant hobbies, and unsupported rankings.

## HR Scorecard

Use this 1-5 rubric before rewriting. Scores are diagnostic, not shown as harsh judgment unless the user asks for a numeric score.

| Dimension | 1 | 3 | 5 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Target Fit | Target role is unclear or conflicts with the content. | Target role is visible, but the top third lacks proof. | Target role is obvious within 8 seconds and backed by matching evidence. |
| Evidence Strength | Bullets describe duties without scope or result. | Some bullets include scope, method, or result. | Most high-value bullets include action, scope, method, and impact. |
| Credibility | Claims are inflated, inconsistent, or unsupported. | Claims are plausible but need tighter proof. | Dates, roles, metrics, and claims are internally consistent and verifiable. |
| Scan Path | Key facts are buried in paragraphs or weak ordering. | Sections are readable but strongest proof is not always first. | The strongest and most relevant proof appears first in each scan path. |
| Keyword Fit | Important role keywords are missing or generic. | Core keywords exist but variants are incomplete. | Keywords match the target role naturally across summary, skills, and bullets. |
| Risk Control | Private data, exaggerated language, or irrelevant content creates concern. | Minor risk exists but can be corrected quickly. | Resume avoids privacy, exaggeration, relevance, and formatting risks. |

Recommended output:

- Brief diagnosis for the top 3 limiting dimensions.
- One high-impact fix per weak dimension.
- Metric questions only for facts that would materially improve positioning.

## Section Strategy

Use the structure that best fits the candidate:

- Experienced candidate: summary, core skills, work experience, selected projects, education, certifications.
- New graduate: profile, education, internships, projects, campus/leadership, skills, awards/certifications.
- Technical role: summary, technical skills, work experience, projects, education, open-source/portfolio.
- Product/operations role: summary, experience, projects, data/analysis tools, education, awards.
- Career transition: summary, transferable skills, relevant projects, prior experience, education/certifications.

## Minimum Input Standard

Before a rewrite or new resume, identify whether the user provided:

- Target role or target direction.
- Existing resume or raw background materials.
- At least one work, internship, project, campus, or portfolio experience.
- Education background and graduation status when relevant.
- Contact fields needed for a final artifact.

If target role and current materials are both missing, ask one concise question for the missing anchor. If a target direction is present but no resume, raw background, or experience material is available, ask for source materials before rewriting or creating resume content. If partial materials are present, proceed only with explicit positioning assumptions and missing-fact notes; do not invent experience, education, metrics, employers, dates, or contact details.

High-value metric questions:

- What scale did the work touch: users, orders, revenue, data rows, documents, interviews, campaigns, stakeholders, or systems?
- What changed after the work: conversion, retention, efficiency, error rate, satisfaction, delivery cycle, completion rate, or cost?
- What was the candidate's exact contribution: owner, core contributor, analyst, coordinator, developer, designer, reviewer, or operator?

## Bullet Rewrite Formula

Write each bullet as:

`Action + object + method/scope + measurable result or business impact`

Examples:

- Weak: "Responsible for user feedback collection."
- Strong: "Maintained 3 user feedback channels, tagged 420+ issues by severity and scenario, and produced weekly reports that helped prioritize 4 product fixes."

- Weak: "Participated in content operations."
- Strong: "Planned 80+ posts for the student media account, tracked title/cover/channel data, and raised repeatable post-review efficiency for a 6-person team."

Chinese examples:

- Weak: "负责活动运营。"
- Strong: "策划并落地 4 场校园拉新活动，覆盖 1,200+ 目标用户，按渠道记录报名、到场与转化数据，并沉淀活动复盘模板。"

- Weak: "参与数据分析。"
- Strong: "整理 8 周用户行为数据，按渠道、场景和转化阶段拆分漏斗，输出 3 条产品优化建议供团队排期评估。"

- Weak: "熟悉 Python 和 SQL。"
- Strong: "使用 SQL 清洗 10 万+ 行业务数据，并用 Python 自动生成周报图表，减少重复整理时间。"

- Weak: "沟通能力强。"
- Strong: "对接产品、设计和运营 3 类角色，跟进需求确认、素材交付和上线检查，保障项目按 2 周周期发布。"

## Metrics Without Fabrication

If exact metrics are missing, use truthful proxies:

- Scale: users, orders, rows, documents, interviews, posts, campaigns, departments.
- Frequency: daily, weekly, monthly, sprint-based, semester-long.
- Complexity: stakeholder count, system count, region count, product line count.
- Quality: error reduction, cycle time, completion rate, satisfaction, retention, conversion.
- Output: reports, dashboards, SOPs, templates, playbooks, experiments, launches.

If no metric or proxy exists, write impact qualitatively and flag a metric question for the user.

## HR Advice Pattern

When giving suggestions, prioritize:

1. Positioning: target role and opening summary.
2. Evidence: strongest achievements and missing metrics.
3. Structure: section order, length, and scan path.
4. Language: verbs, nouns, tense, and redundancy.
5. Screening: keywords, ATS/GEO readability, contact completeness.
6. Risk: unsupported claims, irrelevant details, privacy issues.

## HR Risk Flags

Call out risks when they affect screening decisions:

- Target mismatch: the summary says one role, while experience and skills point to another.
- Contribution ambiguity: team results appear without explaining the candidate's own role.
- Metric credibility: metrics are unusually large, lack context, or conflict with dates and seniority.
- Timeline concerns: gaps, overlaps, very short stints, or unclear graduation dates.
- Keyword mismatch: skills list contains tools that never appear in experience or projects.
- Privacy exposure: ID number, full home address, private client data, internal system names, or confidential metrics.
- Formatting risk: important content appears only in images, badges, icons, or decorative layout.

For each risk, provide a fix phrased as evidence collection or wording adjustment, not as accusation.

## Language Rules

- Use active verbs: led, built, analyzed, launched, improved, automated, coordinated, negotiated, delivered.
- Use role nouns: conversion, retention, funnel, cohort, SQL, dashboard, stakeholder, roadmap, SOP, pipeline.
- Keep bullets usually to 1 line in PDF, 2 lines only for high-value achievements.
- Use consistent tense: present for current roles, past for completed roles.
- Avoid first-person pronouns and full sentences that read like an essay.
